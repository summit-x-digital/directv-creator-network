Format Concepts

Shows, not just posts

DIRECTV is a media company. The creator strategy should think in formats, not just content. These are producible series concepts, backed by Propagate Content's production infrastructure.

Scripted Series

"Home Team" Microdrama

60-90 second episodes. 6-8 per season. Creator-cast, not actor-cast.

Short-form scripted stories built around real DIRECTV moments. A family watching the big game. A veteran coming home to the game on. A Ronald McDonald House family finding comfort in familiar programming. Produced through Propagate. Distributed across TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Reels.

Live Format

"Goals for Good: The Watch Party"

Every goal triggers a visible donation counter. Fans pledge in comments.

Take the existing Goals for Good mechanic and make it participatory entertainment. Creator watches the game, reacts in real-time, donation counter ticks up on screen. Scalable across all 32 markets with local creators. Turns corporate CSR into appointment viewing.

Documentary

"The Giving Room"

5-10 minute interviews. A living room. DIRECTV on in the background.

Longer-form series where creators tell the story of a cause they care about. Tyler Oakley on Trevor Project. Dominic Wakeham on veteran transition. A family creator on their RMHC experience. Warm, editorial, shareable. Propagate produces, DIRECTV distributes.

Charitybuzz x DIRECTV

"Bid for Good"

Celebrity and creator experiences. Only DIRECTV can unlock them.

VIP sports experiences auctioned on Charitybuzz. Watch a game from the booth. Attend a taping. Meet an athlete. Promoted through DIRECTV's subscriber base and creator social channels. Prizeo sweepstakes at $10 entry for mass participation. Proven model: Lin-Manuel Miranda raised $20M+ this exact way.

Three ways to start

Each scenario is a distinct strategy, not a diluted version of the one above it. The right choice depends on what DIRECTV wants to prove first.

4-5 Creators • 90 Days • Proof of Concept
$75K-$125K
